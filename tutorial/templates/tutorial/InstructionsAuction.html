Each employer posts an offer on a platform that is public to all employers and workers. The offer must be between 30 and 100 units, with increments of 5. While an offer is not accepted, the employer can change this standing offer.

A worker can accept any of the standing offers. Once an offer is accepted, it is removed from the platform, and the respective worker and employer are pair up for the remaining stages of the round.

The auction lasts until all employers are matched or for 2 minutes at most. If an offer is not accepted within the 2 minute time window, the employer is not able to hire anyone. Employers (and workers) without a contract will not participate in further stages of the round, but will start again with the next round.

Final wages are subtracted from the employers' payoffs and given to the workers. An employer is however given an endowment of 40 if hiring a worker. The earnings from the real effort task go to the employer. Workers cannot quit the job, after being hired.

An employer without a contract earns 0 units that round. Unemployed workers will earn 20 units (of unemployment benefit). Notice, there are at least 2 workers unemployed each round.

Workers will only know the size of the wage offer - they will not know the identity of their employer. Likewise, the employer does not know whom they have hired.
